# Discovery Process

Use this skill when the user asks for a full discovery plan, opportunity assessment, research-to-decision workflow, or a structured path from vague idea to validated product direction.

Source note: inspired by `deanpeters/Product-Manager-Skills` discovery-process at a high level only. The source license is CC BY-NC-SA 4.0, so this skill is not copied or adapted from its text.

## Workflow

1. Define the decision: what will change after discovery, who decides, and when.
2. Frame the opportunity: target users, problem, current alternatives, business context, constraints, and known signals.
3. List assumptions by risk:
   - Desirability: users need it and will change behavior.
   - Viability: the business outcome is meaningful.
   - Feasibility: the team can deliver and operate it.
   - Usability: users can understand and complete the workflow.
4. Choose evidence methods: interviews, data analysis, prototype tests, fake doors, support review, competitor teardown, or sales/support synthesis.
5. Run discovery in time-boxed loops: question, method, evidence, synthesis, decision.
6. Produce a decision memo: proceed, narrow, pivot, defer, or stop.

## Output

- Discovery objective and decision
- Opportunity framing
- Assumption map
- Research and experiment plan
- Evidence log structure
- Synthesis format
- Decision memo
- Delivery handoff requirements

## Guardrails

- Discovery must support a decision; avoid research for its own sake.
- Do not overfit to stakeholder opinions without user or behavioral evidence.
- If evidence is weak, recommend the cheapest next test before writing a full PRD.
